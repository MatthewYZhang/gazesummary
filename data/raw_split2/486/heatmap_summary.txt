The Andean region has a unique agricultural system for thousands of years. Early inhabitants relied on hunting and gathering, but agriculture allowed settled communities and complex societies to rise. The origins and evolution of this system are multifaceted, including environmental adaptation, technological innovation, and social organization. One of the primary challenges was the diverse and harsh environment of the Andes Mountains, which demanded specific adaptations. Early cultivation began in the lower valleys, where conditions were more favorable for plant growth. The domestication of crops such as potatoes, quinoa, and beans provided a stable food source and formed the basis of the Andean diet.
