Rumors, an integral part of human communication and social dynamics, spread rapidly, shaping perceptions and fueling emotions. The study of rumors offers insights into belief formation and social behavior. A primary reason for their propagation is their ability to address psychological needs, especially in uncertainty, providing a sense of understanding and control. They offer explanations for complex events and attribute blame for negative outcomes.

Rumors can offer a sense of understanding and control in the face of the unknown, addressing psychological needs and satisfying the need for cognitive closure, rumors can become psychologically comforting. The social function of rumors, distortion, source credibility, and emotional context also affect the belief in and sharing of them. Emotional arousal, such as fear or excitement, makes rumors more memorable and impactful. Understanding these dynamics can help us recognize the mechanisms underlying rumor propagation and promote critical thinking.
